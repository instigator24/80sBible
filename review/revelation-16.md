### Revelation 16:1-4
**WEB:** I heard a loud voice out of the temple, saying to the seven angels, “Go and pour out the seven bowls of the wrath of God on the earth!” The first went, and poured out his bowl into the earth, and it became a harmful and evil sore on the people who had the mark of the beast, and who worshiped his image. The second angel poured out his bowl into the sea, and it became blood as of a dead man. Every living thing in the sea died. The third poured out his bowl into the rivers and springs of water, and they became blood.
**Draft:** I heard a voice booming outta the temple, telling the seven angels, "Go pour out the seven bowls of God's wrath on the earth!" The first angel went and dumped his bowl out, and it turned into these grody, vicious sores breaking out on everybody who had the beast's mark and bowed down to its statue. Way harsh. The second angel poured his bowl into the sea, and the whole thing turned to blood, like something outta a corpse -- everything living in that water just died, every last fish and creature. The third angel poured his bowl into the rivers and springs, and they turned to blood too.

They gnawed their tongues because of the pain, and they blasphemed the God of heaven because of their pains and their sores. They didn’t repent of their works. **Draft:** People got scorched something fierce by that heat, and instead of turning around, they cursed God's name, the one who's actually got power over these plagues. People were gnawing on their own tongues from the pain, cursing the God of heaven over their agony and their sores, but still they wouldn't turn from their bogus ways. ### Revelation 16:17-20

A loud voice came out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, “It is done!” There were lightnings, sounds, and thunders; and there was a great earthquake, such as was not since there were men on the earth, so great an earthquake, so mighty. The great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell. Babylon the great was remembered in the sight of God, to give to her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath. Every island fled away, and the mountains were not found.
**Draft:** The seventh angel poured his bowl out into the air, and a huge voice came booming outta the temple in heaven, straight from the throne, saying, "It's done!" Lightning cracked, thunder rolled, voices sounded, and then this earthquake hit -- so massive, so mighty, that nothing like it had gone down since people first walked the earth. The great city split into three chunks, and every city of every nation came crashing down. God remembered Babylon the great, and handed her the cup filled with the wine of his fierce wrath. Every island took off and vanished, and the mountains just weren't there anymore.
