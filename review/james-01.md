### James 1:1-4
**WEB:** James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes which are in the Dispersion: Greetings. Count it all joy, my brothers, when you fall into various temptations, knowing that the testing of your faith produces endurance. Let endurance have its perfect work, that you may be perfect and complete, lacking in nothing.
**Draft:** It's James here, a servant of God and the Lord Jesus Christ, writin' to the twelve tribes scattered all over the place: Greetin's. Count it as pure joy, my brothers, whenever you fall into all kinds of trials, knowin' that testin' your faith builds up endurance. Let that endurance finish its job all the way, so you turn out complete, missin' nothin'.

### James 1:5-8
**WEB:** But if any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives to all liberally and without reproach; and it will be given to him. But let him ask in faith, without any doubting, for he who doubts is like a wave of the sea, driven by the wind and tossed. For that man shouldn’t think that he will receive anything from the Lord. He is a double-minded man, unstable in all his ways.
**Draft:** But if any of you is comin' up short on wisdom, ask God for it — He hands it out generous to everybody, no judgin' involved, and you'll get it. But ask with faith, no second-guessin', 'cause whoever doubts is like a wave out on the ocean, blown around and tossed by the wind. That guy shouldn't figure he's gonna get anything from the Lord. He's double-minded, unstable in every single thing he does.

### James 1:9-12
**WEB:** But let the brother in humble circumstances glory in his high position; and the rich, in that he is made humble, because like the flower in the grass, he will pass away. For the sun arises with the scorching wind, and withers the grass, and the flower in it falls, and the beauty of its appearance perishes. So also will the rich man fade away in his pursuits. Blessed is the man who endures temptation, for when he has been approved, he will receive the crown of life, which the Lord promised to those who love him.
**Draft:** But let the brother who's broke brag about his high standin', and let the rich guy brag about gettin' humbled, 'cause like a flower in the grass, he's gonna fade away. The sun rises with a scorchin' wind, withers the grass, the flower drops off, and its good looks disappear — same deal, the rich guy's gonna fade away right in the middle of chasin' his stuff. Blessed is the guy who hangs tough through temptation, 'cause once he's proven himself, he's gonna score the crown of life, the one the Lord promised everybody who loves Him.

### James 1:13-16
**WEB:** Let no man say when he is tempted, “I am tempted by God,” for God can’t be tempted by evil, and he himself tempts no one. But each one is tempted when he is drawn away by his own lust, and enticed. Then the lust, when it has conceived, bears sin; and the sin, when it is full grown, produces death. Don’t be deceived, my beloved brothers.
**Draft:** Don't let nobody say, when they're gettin' tempted, "God's temptin' me" — 'cause God can't get tempted by evil, and He never tempts nobody Himself. Everybody gets tempted when their own cravings drag 'em off and hook 'em. Then that craving, once it's cooked, gives birth to sin, and that sin, once it's full grown, cranks out death. Don't get conned, my beloved brothers.

### James 1:17-20
**WEB:** Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights, with whom can be no variation, nor turning shadow. Of his own will he gave birth to us by the word of truth, that we should be a kind of first fruits of his creatures. So, then, my beloved brothers, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, and slow to anger; for the anger of man doesn’t produce the righteousness of God.
**Draft:** Every good gift, every perfect gift, comes down from above, straight from the Father of lights, who never changes up or casts shiftin' shadows. He birthed us on His own will through the word of truth, so we'd end up like the first fruits of everything He made. So, my beloved brothers, everybody oughta be quick to listen, slow to talk, slow to get mad — 'cause a person's anger doesn't produce God's righteousness, no way.

### James 1:21-24
**WEB:** Therefore, putting away all filthiness and overflowing of wickedness, receive with humility the implanted word, which is able to save your souls. But be doers of the word, and not only hearers, deluding your own selves. For if anyone is a hearer of the word and not a doer, he is like a man looking at his natural face in a mirror; for he sees himself, and goes away, and immediately forgets what kind of man he was.
**Draft:** So ditch all the grody filth and overflowin' wickedness, and take in, humble-like, the word that's already planted in you, which can save your souls. But be doers of the word, not just listeners, conning yourselves. 'Cause if somebody just listens to the word without doin' nothin' about it, he's like a guy checkin' out his own face in a mirror — he looks, walks away, and forgets what he even looked like right away.

### James 1:25-27

Pure religion and undefiled before our God and Father is this: to visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep oneself unstained by the world.
**Draft:** But whoever looks into the perfect law of freedom and sticks with it, not just listenin' and forgettin', but actually doin' the work — that guy's gonna be blessed in whatever he does. If anybody thinks he's religious while he can't keep his own mouth in check, conning his own heart, his religion's worthless. Pure, spotless religion in front of our God and Father is this: lookin' after orphans and widows in their hard times, and keepin' yourself from gettin' stained up by this world.
