### Galatians 3:1-4
**WEB:** Foolish Galatians, who has bewitched you not to obey the truth, before whose eyes Jesus Christ was openly portrayed among you as crucified? I just want to learn this from you: Did you receive the Spirit by the works of the law, or by hearing of faith? Are you so foolish? Having begun in the Spirit, are you now completed in the flesh? Did you suffer so many things in vain, if it is indeed in vain?
**Draft:** You dipsticks, you Galatians — who cast a spell on you to bail on the truth, when Jesus Christ got painted out plain as day right in front of your faces, crucified and all? I just wanna know one thing: did you get the Spirit by workin' the law, or by hearin' and believin'? Are you really that clueless? You started out in the Spirit, and now you're tryin' to finish up in the flesh? Did you go through all that grief for nothin', if it really is for nothin'?

### Galatians 3:5-8
**WEB:** He therefore who supplies the Spirit to you, and does miracles among you, does he do it by the works of the law, or by hearing of faith? Even as Abraham “believed God, and it was counted to him for righteousness.” Know therefore that those who are of faith, the same are children of Abraham. The Scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, preached the Good News beforehand to Abraham, saying, “In you all the nations will be blessed.”
**Draft:** So the guy who hands you the Spirit and works miracles among you — is He doin' that by law-keepin', or by hearin' and believin'? Same deal as Abraham, who "believed God, and it got counted to him as righteousness." So get this: whoever's runnin' on faith, those are the real children of Abraham. Scripture already saw comin' that God would make the Gentiles right through faith, so it preached the Good News to Abraham ahead of time, sayin', "Through you, every nation's gonna get blessed."

### Galatians 3:9-12
**WEB:** So then, those who are of faith are blessed with the faithful Abraham. For as many as are of the works of the law are under a curse. For it is written, “Cursed is everyone who doesn’t continue in all things that are written in the book of the law, to do them.” Now that no man is justified by the law before God is evident, for, “The righteous will live by faith.” The law is not of faith, but, “The man who does them will live by them.”
**Draft:** So bottom line, whoever's runnin' on faith gets blessed right along with faithful old Abraham. But whoever's leanin' on law-keepin' is stuck under a curse. It's written: "Cursed is everybody who doesn't keep every single thing written in the law book." It's plain as day nobody gets made right with God by the law, 'cause "the righteous will live by faith." The law's got nothin' to do with faith — it's, "whoever does these things will live by 'em."

### Galatians 3:13-16
**WEB:** Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us. For it is written, “Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree,” that the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Christ Jesus; that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith. Brothers, speaking of human terms, though it is only a man’s covenant, yet when it has been confirmed, no one makes it void, or adds to it. Now the promises were spoken to Abraham and to his offspring. He doesn’t say, “To descendants”, as of many, but as of one, “To your offspring”, which is Christ.
**Draft:** Christ bought us back outta the law's curse by becomin' a curse Himself, for us — 'cause it's written, "Cursed is everybody who hangs on a tree." He did it so Abraham's blessing could reach the Gentiles through Christ Jesus, so we could get the promised Spirit through faith. Brothers, talkin' in plain human terms — even just a regular guy's contract, once it's locked in, nobody can void it or tack stuff onto it. Now the promises got spoken to Abraham and his offspring. God didn't say "to descendants," like a whole crowd of 'em — He said "to your offspring," singular, meanin' Christ.

### Galatians 3:17-20
**WEB:** Now I say this: A covenant confirmed beforehand by God in Christ, the law, which came four hundred thirty years after, does not annul, so as to make the promise of no effect. For if the inheritance is of the law, it is no more of promise; but God has granted it to Abraham by promise. Then why is there the law? It was added because of transgressions, until the offspring should come to whom the promise has been made. It was ordained through angels by the hand of a mediator. Now a mediator is not between one, but God is one.
**Draft:** Here's what I'm gettin' at: a deal God already locked in with Christ can't get canceled out by the law, which showed up four hundred thirty years down the road — that wouldn't be fair, killin' the promise like that. 'Cause if the inheritance came from the law, it wouldn't be a promise no more — but God handed it to Abraham straight-up as a promise. So what's the point of the law, then? It got tacked on 'cause of sin, until the offspring the promise was made about finally showed up. It got set up through angels, by a middleman's hand. But a middleman's only needed when there's more than one side — and God's just one.

### Galatians 3:21-24
**WEB:** Is the law then against the promises of God? Certainly not! For if there had been a law given which could make alive, most certainly righteousness would have been of the law. But the Scriptures imprisoned all things under sin, that the promise by faith in Jesus Christ might be given to those who believe. But before faith came, we were kept in custody under the law, confined for the faith which should afterwards be revealed. So that the law has become our tutor to bring us to Christ, that we might be justified by faith.
**Draft:** So is the law fightin' against God's promises? No way, not a chance! 'Cause if a law coulda been handed down that could actually give life, then righteousness woulda come straight from the law, easy. But Scripture locked everything up under sin, so the promise through faith in Jesus Christ could get handed to whoever believes. But before faith showed up, we were locked up under the law, cooped up waitin' for the faith that was gonna get revealed later. So the law turned into our babysitter, draggin' us along toward Christ, so we could get made right through faith.

### Galatians 3:25-28
**WEB:** But now that faith has come, we are no longer under a tutor. For you are all children of God, through faith in Christ Jesus. For as many of you as were baptized into Christ have put on Christ. There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free man, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus.
**Draft:** But now that faith's finally showed up, we don't need that babysitter no more. You're all God's kids now, through faith in Christ Jesus. Every last one of you who got baptized into Christ has suited up in Christ. There's no Jew or Greek in this deal, no slave or free man, no male or female — you're all one, no exceptions, in Christ Jesus.
