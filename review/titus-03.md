### Titus 3:1-4
**WEB:** Remind them to be in subjection to rulers and to authorities, to be obedient, to be ready for every good work, to speak evil of no one, not to be contentious, to be gentle, showing all humility toward all men. For we were also once foolish, disobedient, deceived, serving various lusts and pleasures, living in malice and envy, hateful, and hating one another. But when the kindness of God our Savior and his love toward mankind appeared,

### Titus 3:5-8
**WEB:** not by works of righteousness which we did ourselves, but according to his mercy, he saved us through the washing of regeneration and renewing by the Holy Spirit, whom he poured out on us richly, through Jesus Christ our Savior; that being justified by his grace, we might be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life. This saying is faithful, and concerning these things I desire that you affirm confidently, so that those who have believed God may be careful to maintain good works. These things are good and profitable to men;
**Draft:** not 'cause of righteous stuff we did ourselves, but 'cause of His mercy — He saved us through the washin' of bein' born new and renewed by the Holy Spirit, who He poured out on us rich and generous, through Jesus Christ our Savior; so, made right by His grace, we could become heirs with the hope of eternal life. This sayin's trustworthy, and I want you to say it confidently, so believers make it their business to stay busy with good works. This stuff's good and useful for people;

### Titus 3:9-12
**WEB:** but shun foolish questionings, genealogies, strife, and disputes about the law; for they are unprofitable and vain. Avoid a factious man after a first and second warning, knowing that such a one is perverted and sins, being self-condemned. When I send Artemas to you, or Tychicus, be diligent to come to me to Nicopolis, for I have determined to winter there.
**Draft:** but steer clear of dumb arguments, family trees, fightin', and disputes over the law — that stuff's useless and empty. Avoid somebody stirrin' up cliques after givin' 'em one or two warnin's, knowin' a guy like that's twisted and sinnin', condemnin' himself with his own actions. When I send Artemas or Tychicus your way, hustle to come meet me in Nicopolis, 'cause I already decided to spend the winter there.

### Titus 3:13-15
**WEB:** Send Zenas, the lawyer, and Apollos on their journey speedily, that nothing may be lacking for them. Let our people also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they may not be unfruitful. All who are with me greet you. Greet those who love us in faith. Grace be with you all. Amen.
**Draft:** Send Zenas the lawyer and Apollos off on their trip quick, and make sure they got everything they need. Let our own folks learn to keep busy with good works for whatever's needed, so they don't end up unfruitful. Everybody here with me sends their greetin's. Greet everybody who loves us in the faith. Grace be with all of you. Amen.
