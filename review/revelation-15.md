### Revelation 15:1-4
**WEB:** I saw another great and marvelous sign in the sky: seven angels having the seven last plagues, for in them God’s wrath is finished. I saw something like a sea of glass mixed with fire, and those who overcame the beast, his image, and the number of his name, standing on the sea of glass, having harps of God. They sang the song of Moses, the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, “Great and marvelous are your works, Lord God, the Almighty! Righteous and true are your ways, you King of the nations. Who wouldn’t fear you, Lord, and glorify your name? For you only are holy. For all the nations will come and worship before you. For your righteous acts have been revealed.”
**Draft:** Then I saw another huge, epic sign up in the sky -- seven angels holding the seven last plagues, the ones that finally wrap up God's wrath for good. I also saw what looked like a sea of glass all mixed up with fire, and standing right there on that glassy sea were the ones who'd beaten the beast, beaten its statue, beaten the number of its name -- and every one of 'em was holding a harp from God. They're singing the song of Moses, God's own servant, and the song of the Lamb, belting out, "Your works are great and totally epic, Lord God, the Almighty! Your ways are righteous and true, you King over every nation. Who wouldn't stand in awe of you, Lord, and give glory to your name? You're the only one who's holy. Every nation's gonna come and bow down before you, 'cause your righteous acts are out there for the whole world to see."

### Revelation 15:5-8
**WEB:** After these things I looked, and the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened. The seven angels who had the seven plagues came out, clothed with pure, bright linen, and wearing golden sashes around their breasts. One of the four living creatures gave to the seven angels seven golden bowls full of the wrath of God, who lives forever and ever. The temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power. No one was able to enter into the temple, until the seven plagues of the seven angels would be finished.
**Draft:** After all that, I looked again and the temple -- the tabernacle of testimony up in heaven -- swung wide open. The seven angels carrying the seven plagues stepped out, decked in pure, bright linen with gold sashes wrapped around their chests, looking righteous and fierce. One of the four living creatures handed each of the seven angels a golden bowl, and every bowl was brimming full with the wrath of God, the one who lives forever and ever. Smoke from God's own glory and power filled up the whole temple, so thick that nobody -- not one single soul -- could step inside until the seven angels finished pouring out their seven plagues.
